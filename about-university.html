Overview of UUHF

Shaping Green Futures in Horticulture & Forestry

Established in 2011 by the Government of Uttarakhand (Act No. 8 of 2011), the Uttarakhand University of Horticulture and Forestry (UUHF) is a premier institution dedicated to excellence in agricultural sciences, with a specialized focus on horticulture, forestry, and allied disciplines. The university is headquartered at Bharsar, Pauri Garhwal, nestled in the scenic Himalayan foothills. UUHF was carved out of the historic G.B. Pant University of Agriculture & Technology to cater specifically to the unique agro-climatic and forestry needs of the Uttarakhand region. The university aims to foster sustainable natural resource management, biodiversity conservation, and rural prosperity through cutting-edge research and industry-relevant education.

Our Vision

To become a globally recognized center of excellence in horticulture and forestry education, research, and extension, contributing to food security, ecological balance, and sustainable livelihood development.

Our Mission

To produce highly skilled professionals; conduct need-based research in horticulture, forestry, and allied sciences; promote technology transfer; and conserve the rich Himalayan biodiversity through participatory approaches.

Recognized by UGC, ICAR, and accredited by NAAC with 'A' Grade, UUHF offers a wide range of undergraduate, postgraduate, and doctoral programs. The university has two constituent colleges: College of Horticulture (Bharsar) and College of Forestry (Ranichauri), along with Krishi Vigyan Kendras (KVKs) and research stations across the state.

At a Glance: Institutional Legacy

UUHF functions with a mandate to advance horticulture and forestry sectors through academic rigor and field-level extension. The university has significantly contributed to the development of high-yielding varieties of mandarin, pear, and medicinal plants adapted to the Himalayan region. With sprawling experimental farms, a modern tissue culture facility, and a central library housing over 25,000 volumes, the campus provides an invigorating atmosphere for learning. The university also hosts the Centre for Himalayan Biodiversity and Climate Change Studies, which collaborates with national and international agencies.

Moreover, UUHF has signed MoUs with several ICAR institutes, Forest Research Institute (FRI), and international universities to foster student exchange and joint research. The university's placement cell ensures that graduates are recruited by leading horticulture industries, banking sectors, and state forest departments. More than 2,000 alumni are currently serving as scientists, entrepreneurs, and policymakers across the country.

Meet Our Visionary Administrators

Prof. (Dr.) Anil Kumar Shukla

Vice Chancellor, UUHF

Dr. Anil Kumar Shukla is a distinguished academician with over 30 years of experience in horticulture research and higher education administration. Under his visionary leadership, UUHF has strengthened its research output, forged international collaborations, and implemented outcome-based education models. He has been instrumental in launching the 'Himalayan Horticulture Innovation Hub' and expanding scholarship programs for meritorious students from rural backgrounds. His areas of expertise include fruit science, post-harvest management, and policy planning for sustainable mountain agriculture.
